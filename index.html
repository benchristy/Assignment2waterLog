Our Project

Plans and Progress

How it started:
As a very diverse group we naturally have a varying array of hobbies between us. One of our members, Max, had large fish tanks filled with exotic fish. This initial inspiration nurtured the idea of an application to give the correct information about the water in your area/city.
We created waterlog as a way the anyone could obtain all the information that they could possibly need about your local water.

What it will do?
waterLog will be an application for both mobile phones and computer, this application will be free of charge to users. The focus for waterLog is to provide legitimate and relevant data surrounding water to the public, to inform them of relevant information of the water quality near them.
The design focus of the waterLog application is to provide all necessary data in a clean, professional, user oriented and easily understandable design.
All applications offered by waterLog will have the same GUI, unless decided against in the future. waterLog's GUI will include multiple tabs including FAQ, settings and notifications. The core of the waterLog GUI will be a map of the user entered country, in which they can click on cities/states to see the water data for that given area.

Users will be prompted on the first launch of the waterLog application to either allow location tracking, or enter their location manually. This is so we can present the relevant map on their screen, and to provide them with notifications regarding their location. The user can decide to manually input their location, or even leave it blank and use the search bar to find the data they are seeking.
Currently the computer application is the only service we offer, however we are planning on creating the mobile phone variants in the coming weeks. The mobile phone variants will include both IOS and Android operating systems. Data for Melbourne's water quality is the only current information we present to users, as this is our ‘beta’ stage of production. With the creation of the waterLog mobile phone applications, we will be adding more data for different regions in the world.

The application consists of a database – currently made using Java, however as the data increases, we will move to a database focused language such a SQL.
The database will store all data for each region of the world we offer our services to, the database was built so that when a user clicks on a city/state the information regarding that area is shown. The database will be maintained by its creator, Andrew Rijs, with all up-to-date information sourced from government or water treatment plant reports and websites.
waterLog will also have a FAQ page, in which will list many beneficial facts and tips for users from many different professions/hobbies. Users will have the option to create accounts with waterLog, with this they can save multiple preferred locations to quickly ‘travel’ to them on the map. Users with accounts can also sign up to receive emails from our developers and team members, with updates on any new updates coming to waterLog and helpful information regarding water.






waterLog will be hosted using a PaaS plan, as our current team doesn’t have the hardware or premises to use our own servers.
waterLog will be an application that is free to use, that will inform users of water data in regions across the world. Our goal at waterLog is to promote the education surrounding relevant water data and information. Water data is relevant for many professions, health, leisure and appliance needs, as a result millions of people can find use out of our app. waterLog will be completely transparent regarding user privacy, ensuring your data is always protected. Due to waterLog being a free application, the money generated will be through promoting advertisement on our platforms. WaterLog will never sell any user related data, user data such as location will only be used to show notifications. Other than notifications all other data is simply unneeded, therefore we disregard it after assigning users to the chosen location. Our database will be fully encrypted and protected from leading cyber security experts.

waterLog's aim is to provide the general public with a source for easily accessible data surrounding water, this is relevant to many fields and leisure activities. waterLog will also provide information surrounding water through an FAQ tab in the application. Once waterLog is out of the ‘beta’ stage and starts gaining more countries data, this application can be used to potentially save human lives. With many countries not having easily accessible clean drinking water, this application can help tourists determine where they can or can’t drink. waterLog aims to do this all free to the end user, only gaining revenue through advertisements and donations, while also ensuring all user data is encrypted and protected.

How will it do it?
The waterLog application will be made for pc and ios/android devices. The application consists of a simple, yet professional looking GUI in which any user should understand its use immediately. The core aspect of this GUI is an interactive map, of which when the user selects a region the stats of available water management systems will be shown.
Upon initial launch of the waterLog applications, all data will be stored in a Java database. However, as the userbase and data grows over time, a more fitting SQL database will be created. Using this Java database, the GUI presents the user with reliable and up-to date data.
In addition to the MVP java database/ui we will be making an Entity-Relationship Model diagram as the next step before creating an SQL database.
ER Models are what you use to conceptualize and define key elements of data before committing to creating the database and includes entities (ie. 'Location ID', 'Location', 'Information Source', 'pH', 'Fluoride Content'), keys and relationships as core concepts.
The diagram we make will use these concepts, as well as crow's feet notation to map out the transition to a fully-functioning database.

Project Description


waterLog will be an application in which people can obtain pertinent water parameters for most main cities in Australia, later this will be expanded to most of the world's countries.
The water parameters presented to the user will be derived from the destination’s readily available tap water and all data will be gained through government and/or official company sources and databases.

Overview

Topic
The parameters provided to the user from the waterLog application will include, general water hardness, PH, chlorine, trace elements and more.
Water is the most crucial resource to humans and animal-kind alike, these parameters will potentially assist a vast variety of people, those with health concerns, those wanting to gain the best from their home appliances (some minerals in water can cause build up on and damage kitchen appliances), professional fields (hydroponics, construction), and animal lovers, especially aquatic.
waterLog will source and collate the historical and current data reported by each major city’s multiple water management companies, we will compile all relevant information into a database to be deployed in a smart phone application and website. The data will be presented in a clean, professional looking user-oriented design. The focus will be to provide users with appropriate data in an instantaneous and uncomplicated way.

Motivation
The motivation behind waterLog came from one of our group members owning fish, Max Torti had recently purchased a fish tank and pet fish, so become interested in maintaining their optimal environmental conditions.
He discovered fish required varying water parameters, generally related to where they were naturally found (e.g. fish from Africa require harder and less acidic water). To modify the water in his fish tank to ensure it best suited his new fish, Max needed accurate, current data on the water. Max discovered gaining this information was more complicated than he had expected, he searched multiple websites and applications to find the information available was lengthy scripts of data, unreliably sourced or not specific to his area. He soon conceded and bought himself a water test kit.

Landscape
We have been unable to find a website or application which provides similar water data tailored to the purposes we have outlined. Water Management companies around Australia have information available on their respective websites, however this data is generally in the form of a downloaded PDF with pages and pages of information.
There is a smart phone application which provides water catchment information, which provides data on the amount of water in our drinking catchments, not so much the mineral components of the water.
There are applications for detecting water underground or reminding users to drink the recommended amount of water each day for health purposes.
A website similar to our proposed application is https://water.australianmap.net/physical_chemical/hardness/
However, this website does not deliver the scope of parameters, collate or present the data to the user for a specific purpose and in a simple and targeted way, so we conclude there to be no comparable applications currently on the market and thus no competition.

Aims
Water is the most important resource to humans; we rely upon it for survival, from drinking to growing our food sources.
waterLog aims to provide accessibility of accurate water data. We aim to deliver an easy to use platform which can be relied upon to provide valid data from reliable sources, at no cost to the user.

Priority Goals

Our priority will initially be to provide data for water in Melbourne, Australia. We intend to provide the most relevant information effecting common water-based activities. Elements included in this data will be:
• PH Levels
• Colour (Pt/Co)
• Iron
• Manganese
• Fluoride
• Calcium
• Hardness
• Magnesium
• Nitrate
• Alkalinity
*The provision of this data may vary from time to time and be dependent on the most recent testing results.
We then hope to grow this data to a national level, covering all major cities in Australia. Australia is lucky enough to count most of its main water systems as safe and consumable. However, those who travel within Australia, will agree there are differing water taste between the cities. waterLog will break down the chemical components causing these taste variations (Hardness, PH, Calcium, Magnesium, Sodium and Chlorine can all cause changes in waters taste, colour or even texture). Our end goal for this application is to provide easily accessible data from across the world, especially in places that lack clean drinking water.
waterLog data will clearly distinguish a locations quality of water and its safe consumption for humans. Locals and travellers equally, will find value in the data.

Scopes and Limits

Our scope is to create a simple application which allows users to see the water parameters and conditions in their current location (taken from their GPS signal) or a location of their choosing. Imminently, we are planning our focus on Melbourne, Australia, to ensure we have the capacity to identify and remedy any application glitches or limitations.
Once streamlined, we will broaden our data field to include significant locations in Australia and the world. Gathering this data will prove involved, so will require substantial resources.

Tools and Technologies

Once the application is downloaded to a user’s phone, it will ask permission to obtain the user’s position, using GPS as to address when notification is sent. The user can opt in or out of the GPS, if for example the user opts out, they can manually set their city/destination via text or speech or just leave it blank.
Notifications will appear whenever there is a change in water quality, water containment and delivery infrastructure or contaminations in a user’s selected area.

Notifications will be sent depending on the user's location, as the notifications will be surrounding changes to the user’s water management system of their city/state. If the user doesn’t enter their destination or enable GPS, the notifications will not be sent, however they can view all notifications in the app itself under the “notifications” tab.

Team Profile
Our team has taken various personality tests to help indicate how we will function together . A Myers-Briggs test by 16 Personalities reveals that we have a Protagonist, an Architect, a Logician, a Virtuoso and two Mediators. With this information, we can speculate on how the group may function as the project unfolds.

The Protagonist would take a leadership and organisation role, especially in the initial formation of the group. The Architect and Logician may come up with the initial blueprints of the idea, with the Virtuoso also having significant input.

Off of the cusp, abstract ideas will often be contributed by the mediators and the Virtuoso, as well as other members of the team. The mediators and the protagonist will also be available to help the Architect, Logician and Virtuoso in communicating their ideas effectively with the group.

The team has also individually taken a combination of learning styles tests, which has shown that we have a broad mix of learning preferences. The groups balance of auditory, visual, kinaesthetic and other learning styles will give us a healthy range of ideas and problem solving techniques to utilise from.

Finally, a big 5 test reveals that our team is quite mixed in makeup. Most members are at partially extroverted, however some are not. This will mean that our team must be conscious of being inclusive with sharing input, and asking everybody for their opinions. Most team members are generally quite agreeable, which will hopefully mean collaboration is easy for us during group meetings. Most of our team members scored highly in openness/imagination and will be able to contribute creative ideas to our final project.

Overall our group has a strong mix of personalities and learning methods which will allow for a diverse range of ideas and opinions. Our hope is that this group dynamic is rich enough that everyone will have had a strong role to play in the overall project.

Ideal Jobs

With Information Technology being such a broad field, there is room for many types of people to have a role in the industry. The waterLog team has a diverse range of personality types. As such, it seems fitting that we would all have very different plans for our career paths.

Our ideal jobs have some soft skills in common. As IT is a teamwork based industry, communication skills are a highly sought after skill in most roles. Most of our ideal jobs also have a strong emphasis on problem solving and analytical skills.

The technical skillsets for each job we have selected are unique from each other. The programmer roles overlap in many ways however there are differences between Front End and PHP Developers. While the Front will generally focus on visual development, The PHP developer is more likely to focus their work in the back end, and be highly proficient in SQL.PHP and Front-End developers will both generally be competent in JavaScript.

The business analyst role is unique in that it is a hybrid role, i.e implementing technology to utilise a business skillset. Data analysis is something that a business analyst will need to be proficient in, which broadly overlaps with with PHP development (As SQL is a language used for data management). Data Centre Technicians and Network engineers, also handle data but in a very different way from an analyst. These roles more focus on the administration, maintenance and handling of data networks. Both will generally have experience working in data centres.

Cyber security is unique to the rest of our job choices, as it is centred around protecting data from intentional threats. However there is some overlap with the Developer roles in terms of being tailored to businesses needs and designing applications accordingly.
